Phase 1 — Core Line

Annelinn ↔ Kesklinn ↔ Maarjamõisa ↔ Lõunakeskus

The backbone corridor connecting Tartu's largest residential district, the city centre, the university hospital, and the main southern retail hub. Single continuous line, ~12 km, ~20 stops. Includes depot construction and initial fleet of 9 LRVs. Mandatory traffic signal priority from day one.

Phase 2 — Raadi Extension

Kesklinn → ERM / Raadi district

Extends from the city centre east to the Estonian National Museum (ERM) and the developing Raadi district. Opens only if Phase 1 exceeds 10 000 daily boardings by 2033. ~6 km addition, ~8 new stops. Reuses Phase 1 depot and supplements fleet by 3–4 vehicles.

2040+
Phase 3 — Regional Extension

Lõunakeskus → Ülenurme → Tartu Airport

A regional extension to Ülenurme municipality and Tartu Airport. Viable only if Ülenurme suburban density increases substantially and airport traffic grows beyond 500 000 pax/year. Until then, a dedicated express bus to the airport is the right solution. ~5 km, deferred indefinitely.

Geospatial Recommendations
Maximise Cost-Effectiveness

Eight actions that determine whether this project achieves world-class value-for-money or becomes an expensive underperformer.

🛤️
Phase 1 Only First
The Annelinn–Kesklinn–Maarjamõisa–Lõunakeskus corridor carries ~80% of potential network demand. Build only this. Validate ridership before committing to Phases 2 or 3.
🚦
Mandatory Signal Priority
Traffic Signal Priority (TSP) raises commercial speed from 18 to 22+ km/h. This is the single highest-ROI investment in the entire system — cost ~€2M, benefit multiplies ridership appeal permanently.
🌿
Green Track on Riia maantee
Grass-embedded track on the wide Riia maantee median costs 10–15% more than paved track, but measurably increases induced ridership and property uplift in comparable European cities.
🚌
Restructure Bus Network Day 1
Remove parallel bus lines on the tram corridor on opening day. Redirect freed bus capacity to feeder routes. Zero additional cost; improves tram ridership by ~15% and reduces total public transport subsidy.
🇪🇺
Secure ≥75% EU Funding
BCR clears the 1.0 threshold only with EU co-financing. Target: EU Cohesion Fund + TEN-T Urban Nodes in the 2028–2034 programme period. Tartu qualifies as a TEN-T Core Network corridor urban node.
🔲
At-Grade Only
Reject any proposal to go underground. Underground sections cost 4–8× more per km. Tartu has no topographic or traffic reason to tunnel. Any underground proposal is a cost blocker, not an upgrade.
🌉
Use Vabadussild Crossing
Vabadussild offers the most direct river crossing to Annelinn. Structural review required, but modifications are substantially cheaper than a new dedicated tram bridge. Estimate: €3–5M works vs €20M+ for a new bridge.
✈️
Defer Airport Branch
Tartu Airport handles ~200 000 pax/year — far below the threshold for tram viability. An express bus is correct here. The Riga–Tartu–Tallinn rail link (operational Jan 2026) is the regional transport priority, not airport light rail.
